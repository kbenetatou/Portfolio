An AR Experience at the V&A’s Theatre & Performance Gallery

Opportunity & Our Goal

While exploring the V&A Museum, we noticed that the Theatre collection on the second floor was:

  • Under-visited compared to other areas
  • Lacking interactivity, despite visually rich content

We saw an opportunity to engage users more deeply with costumes through AR try-on technology, storytelling, and playful interactions.

Our goal was to create an interactive AR installation that allows visitors to virtually try on theatrical costumes, act out a scene, and explore rich behind-the-scenes content — without physically handling fragile garments.

User Research

Methods:

  • Naturalistic observations at the V&A Theatre Gallery
  • User interviews (in-person and phone)
  • Thematic analysis using affinity mapping and empathy maps

Key Findings:

  • Visitors enjoyed the costumes but wanted more context and interactivity
  • Navigation within the exhibit was confusing
  • Concerns about hygiene discouraged physical interaction
  • Users responded well to visual learning and storytelling

Personas

  • Eric – Theatre Enthusiast
    Wants detailed costume history, deeper learning, and interactive ways to connect.
  • Eleanor – Casual Museum-Goer
    Feels overwhelmed by static displays and prefers playful, visually engaging learning.

Ideation & Concept Development

We explored various ideas — including sound-based sensors and VR theatre scenes — but filtered them through criteria like inclusivity, safety, and connection to the museum context.

Final concept:
An AR Try-On Station with features like:

  • Virtual costume fitting (e.g. Shirley Bassey’s gown)
  • Act-it-Out: record yourself performing a scene
  • Changeable backgrounds inspired by theatre & film
  • Costume details, videos, behind-the-scenes content
  • Easy sharing via email

Prototype Walkthrough

Built in Figma, the prototype simulates the experience on a life-sized screen in the museum.

Key Features:

  • Start screen with navigation help
  • AR scanning guidance
  • Virtual costume try-on
  • Photo capture & background swap (inspired by Snapchat filters)
  • Act-It-Out video creation with a countdown and script
  • Behind-the-scenes costume info
  • Email sharing of photo/video

Usability Testing

What Worked:

  • Easy-to-use, intuitive interaction
  • Exciting & memorable experience
  • Loved the photo and act-it-out features

What We Improved:

  • Made the “Start” button more visible
  • Clarified that photos weren’t stored (privacy concerns)
  • Added lighting guidance due to dim gallery space
  • Labeled confusing icons (e.g. “Act it out”)
